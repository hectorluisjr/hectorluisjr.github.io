Sensory and Connection

Looking out the window, you see the rain start to fall as the sun still shines through. In excitement, you run outside barefoot. You feel the wet grass and mud as you give the earth little toe hugs. The crisp refreshing smells of nature fill your nose with every inhale. The rain feels like it is dancing on your skin as you twirl and tilt your head back, listening to gentle thunder in the background.

Imaginative, Pretend and Objects

Yesterday you witnessed a spaceship launch into outer space. Scanning your surroundings in curiosity you see a couch, 4 big pillows, a fake snake, a box and a plate. Today, you are going to space! Gathering your supplies you assemble the pillow roof, the box becomes your passageway, the snake is your seatbelt and the plate is your steering wheel. Blast off!

Kinesthetic, Body and Movement

Rising onto your toes, you begin to twirl in a circle, arms widespread as you see the room shift colors, faster and faster, colors meld into a blur. As you stop, you feel your feet lead you sideways quickly heading towards the couch. Laying down, eyes open, watching the ceiling as it continues to move. After a few seconds it comes to a stop. You feel the buzzing energy course through your body as you lay there noticing your heavy breathing soften. Relaxed and smiling.

Playing with Stories

Going to the book shelf, you see row after row of brightly colored books. On the covers there are animals, trucks and cars, and mythical creatures. Your hand grazes over the books before pulling your favorite one out. Walking over to the couch with your teddy bear, you sit down and are wrapped in a blanket. A gentle and expressive voice begins to read. You take in the many colors and excitement of the story unfolding. You imagine being a part of the story and memorize each line. You are immersed and present.

Our Founder

“Kai” Kayla Arnone is guided in the creation of this practice by all the children who have taught her the magic of child-led play and what it means to delight in the present moment.

Since gaining her psychology degree, Kai has spent the past decade exploring our inner connections with childhood — how playfulness releases trauma, heals our bodies, and rekindles our potential for wondrous growth. She has learned from infants and children through childcare the enrichment of being immersed in play. Her practice is grounded by trainings under leading scholars in somatic healing, holistic circus therapy, and trauma treatment.

I live my dream... guiding hundreds of international collaborations with individual clients and families on their playful healing journeys so far.
